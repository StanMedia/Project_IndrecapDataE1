Palliative care can improve quality of life for people with dementia

Kindel Media/pexels

A new article discusses the benefits and challenges of palliative care for people with dementia and their caregivers.

What is palliative care and how can it help people with dementia?

Palliative care is a type of care that focuses on relieving the symptoms and improving the quality of life of people with serious illnesses, such as dementia. Palliative care can be provided at any stage of the disease, not just at the end of life. Palliative care can help people with dementia by:

  • Managing pain, discomfort, agitation, anxiety, depression and other physical and emotional symptoms
  • Providing support and education to caregivers and family members
  • Addressing spiritual and cultural needs and preferences
  • Helping with advance care planning and decision making
  • Coordinating care among different health professionals and services

Palliative care can be delivered by a team of specialists, such as doctors, nurses, social workers, chaplains and volunteers, who work together with the person's primary care provider. Palliative care can be provided in various settings, such as hospitals, nursing homes, hospices or at home.

What are the challenges of palliative care for people with dementia?

Palliative care for people with dementia can also pose some challenges, such as:

  • Recognizing when a person with dementia needs palliative care, as dementia is a progressive and unpredictable disease that affects each person differently
  • Communicating with a person with dementia who may have difficulties with memory, language, reasoning and behavior
  • Respecting the person's wishes and values, especially if they have not expressed them in advance or if they change over time
  • Balancing the benefits and burdens of treatments and interventions, such as hospitalizations, tube feeding, antibiotics or CPR
  • Supporting caregivers and family members who may experience stress, grief, guilt or conflict

How can palliative care be improved for people with dementia?

The article suggests some ways to improve palliative care for people with dementia, such as:

  • Increasing awareness and education about palliative care among health professionals, caregivers and the public
  • Developing guidelines and tools to assess and manage symptoms and quality of life for people with dementia
  • Encouraging early and ongoing conversations about goals of care and preferences among people with dementia, caregivers, family members and health professionals
  • Providing more training and support to caregivers and family members who are often the main source of care for people with dementia
  • Expanding access to palliative care services in different settings and regions

The article concludes that palliative care can offer a person-centered and holistic approach to care for people with dementia that can enhance their dignity, comfort and well-being.
