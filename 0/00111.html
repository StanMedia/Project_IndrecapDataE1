Dubai to pioneer 3D-printing technology with world's first 3D-printed mosque

Adnan Uddin/pexels

The emirate aims to become a global leader in 3D-printing by building a unique and innovative religious structure

Dubai is known for its ambitious and futuristic projects, such as the world's tallest building, the Burj Khalifa, and the world's largest indoor theme park, IMG Worlds of Adventure. Now, the emirate is set to add another remarkable achievement to its list: the world's first 3D-printed mosque.

What is 3D-printing and why is it used?

3D-printing is a technology that creates physical objects from digital models by depositing layers of material on top of each other. It can be used to produce various items, such as furniture, clothing, jewelry, and even food. 3D-printing has several advantages over conventional manufacturing methods, such as:

  • Saving time and resources by reducing waste and transportation costs
  • Enabling customization and personalization of products
  • Enhancing creativity and innovation by allowing complex and intricate designs
  • Improving sustainability and environmental impact by using renewable and recyclable materials

How will the 3D-printed mosque be built?

The 3D-printed mosque will be constructed by the Islamic Affairs and Charitable Activities Department (IACAD) of the Dubai Government, in collaboration with a private company that has not been disclosed yet. The project will be located in Bur Dubai, one of the oldest neighborhoods in the emirate, and will cover an area of 2,000 square meters over two floors. It will be able to accommodate 600 worshippers at a time.

The mosque will be made from a concrete mix that will be extruded from a large printing machine that will follow a pre-programmed design. The printing process will take about six months, while the finishing touches, such as painting and landscaping, will take another six months. The mosque is expected to be completed and opened to the public in the first quarter of 2025.

According to Ali Mohammad Alhalyan Alsuwaidi, head of engineering at IACAD, the decision to use 3D-printing for the mosque was based on its potential to save time and resources compared to traditional building methods. He also said that the mosque will have a distinctive architectural style that will blend Islamic elements with modern technology.

What are Dubai's goals and achievements in 3D-printing?

Dubai has set out to become the 3D-printing capital of the world, and in 2018 it launched a "3D Printing Strategy" that planned for 25% of the emirate's new construction to be 3D-printed by 2030. The strategy aims to promote innovation, economic growth, and social development in various sectors, such as construction, medical, consumer products, and education.

By 2019, Dubai had already achieved several milestones in 3D-printing, such as:

  • Building the world's largest 3D-printed structure: the Dubai Municipality building (standing 9.5 meters tall with an area of 640 square meters)
  • Building the world's first 3D-printed office: the Office of the Future (covering an area of 250 square meters)
  • Building a 3D-printed drone research laboratory: part of the Mohammed Bin Rashid Space Centre (covering an area of 168 square meters)

Dubai is also planning to build more 3D-printed structures in the future, such as:

  • A hotel with 80% of its components being 3D-printed
  • A museum dedicated to showcasing 3D-printing innovations
  • A residential complex with 10% of its units being 3D-printed

How does Dubai compare to other countries in 3D-printing?

Dubai is not the only place in the world that is experimenting with 3D-printing technology. Several other countries have also been using it for various purposes, such as:

  • Providing housing for refugees in Jordan and for homeless people in Austin, Texas
  • Creating whole complexes of buildings, such as the Camp Swift military training center in Texas
  • Developing entire neighborhoods, such as the New Story project in Tabasco, Mexico
  • Exploring new frontiers, such as preparing to build on the lunar surface

What are the benefits and challenges of 3D-printing?

3D-printing technology has many benefits for the construction industry and society at large, such as:

  • Reducing the environmental impact of construction by using less material, energy, and water, and by producing less waste and emissions
  • Improving the quality and durability of buildings by eliminating human errors and defects
  • Enhancing the safety and efficiency of workers by reducing the need for manual labor and heavy machinery
  • Increasing the accessibility and affordability of housing by lowering the cost and time of production and delivery
  • Fostering social inclusion and diversity by enabling people to design and build their own homes according to their needs and preferences

However, 3D-printing technology also faces some challenges and limitations, such as:

  • Finding suitable materials that can withstand different weather conditions and meet building codes and standards
  • Developing reliable and scalable printing machines that can operate in different environments and handle complex designs
  • Ensuring the security and privacy of digital data and models that are used for 3D-printing
  • Addressing the legal and ethical issues that may arise from 3D-printing, such as intellectual property rights, liability, and regulation
  • Managing the social and economic impact of 3D-printing on the existing construction industry and workforce

What is the main purpose of the mosque?

The main purpose of the mosque is to serve as a place where Muslims can come together for prayer. Prayer is the most important act of worship in Islam, and it is obligatory for Muslims to perform it five times a day at prescribed times. The mosque provides a clean and spacious environment for Muslims to perform their prayers in congregation, which has more rewards than praying individually. The mosque also facilitates the performance of other obligatory prayers, such as the Friday prayer (Jumu'ah), the Eid prayers, and the funeral prayer (Janazah).

Prayer is not the only function of the mosque, however. The mosque also serves as a place where Muslims can learn about their religion, celebrate special occasions, give charity, and meet fellow believers. The mosque is a symbol of Muslim unity and identity, and it reflects the values and principles of Islam. The mosque is also a place where Muslims can seek guidance and support from their leaders and scholars, who deliver sermons and lectures on various topics related to Islam. The mosque is a source of spiritual and moral inspiration for Muslims, and it reminds them of their duties and responsibilities towards Allah s.w.t. and His creation.

Conclusion

Dubai is leading the way in 3D-printing technology by building the world's first 3D-printed mosque. The project is part of the emirate's 3D Printing Strategy that aims to transform the construction sector and society as a whole. 3D-printing technology has many advantages over conventional methods, but it also poses some challenges that need to be overcome. Dubai's 3D-printed mosque will be a landmark of innovation and inspiration for the world.
