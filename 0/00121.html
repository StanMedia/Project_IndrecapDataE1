Wagner leader announces withdrawal from Bakhmut amid ammo crisis

The head of the Russian-backed private military company says his men have nothing left to fight with after taking heavy losses in Ukraine.

Wagner's role in Ukraine conflict

Wagner is a private military company that has been involved in various conflicts around the world, such as Syria, Libya, and the Central African Republic. The company is widely believed to be linked to the Kremlin and its founder, Yevgeny Prigozhin, is a close associate of Russian President Vladimir Putin.

Wagner has taken on a growing role in the Ukraine conflict as Russian forces falter in their attempt to annex the eastern part of the country. Wagner has claimed credit for territorial gains, particularly in the battles raging around the eastern Ukrainian city of Bakhmut, which is strategically located near the border with Russia.

Prigozhin's challenge to Russian defense officials

However, as Wagner's stature has increased, so too have its clashes with Russian defense officials, who have been accused by Prigozhin of failing to support and supply his forces in Ukraine. Prigozhin has also blamed defense chiefs for "tens of thousands" of Wagner casualties and accused them of "treason".

In February, Prigozhin publicly challenged Russian Defense Minister Sergei Shoigu and chief of the Russian armed forces Gen. Valery Gerasimov, prompting speculation about possible elite infighting in Moscow as Russia's military campaign fails to advance.

Last week, Prigozhin released an expletive-laden video footage in which he criticized the Russian military command for not providing enough ammunition and equipment to his men. He also said that he was ready to withdraw from Bakhmut if he did not receive any communication from them.

Prigozhin's announcement of withdrawal from Bakhmut

On Saturday, Prigozhin made his announcement official on his Telegram channel. He said that his men would begin withdrawing from Bakhmut this week, because dwindling ammunition supplies and mounting losses mean there is "nothing left to grind the meat with".

He said that Wagner had taken 95% of Bakhmut and that the remaining 5% was not worth fighting for. He said that Wagner's role in Bakhmut had been to grind down Ukrainian forces while allowing Russian forces to regroup.

He also said that he was disappointed by the lack of support from Russia and that it was better to save his army than to win a battle.

Implications of Wagner's withdrawal from Bakhmut

Wagner's withdrawal from Bakhmut could have significant implications for the Ukraine conflict and the wider geopolitical situation. It could signal a weakening of Russia's influence and control over its proxy forces in eastern Ukraine. It could also expose Wagner's vulnerabilities and limitations as a private military company.

On the other hand, it could also create an opportunity for dialogue and de-escalation between Russia and Ukraine, as well as their international partners. It could also reduce the human cost of the conflict, which has claimed more than 13,000 lives since 2014.

Wagner's impact on the Ukraine war

Wagner's involvement in the Ukraine war has had a significant impact on the course and outcome of the conflict. Wagner has been accused of committing atrocities and human rights violations against Ukrainian civilians and soldiers, such as the Bucha killings, where dozens of Ukrainian prisoners were executed by Wagner fighters in March 2022. Wagner has also been instrumental in advancing Russia's territorial ambitions in eastern Ukraine, especially in capturing strategic locations such as Bakhmut and Soledar. However, Wagner's brutal tactics and high casualties have also provoked international condemnation and sanctions, as well as resistance and resentment from the local population. Wagner's role in the Ukraine war has also exposed the rifts and tensions within the Russian political and military elite, as Prigozhin has repeatedly challenged and criticized the Russian defense officials for their lack of support and coordination.
