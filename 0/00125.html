Polish mayor dies in tragic fall from hotel window in Italy

Radosław Grzegorz Ostakiewicz/Wiki

A 44-year-old Polish mayor was visiting Sardinia with a delegation when he fell from the fourth floor of a hotel in Cagliari.

The incident

The mayor, whose name has not been disclosed by the authorities, was staying at the Due Colonne hotel in the historic center of Cagliari, the capital of Sardinia. According to the Polish Press Agency (PAP) and the spokesperson for Fabrizio Mustaro, the investigator of the Cagliari police, the mayor was sitting on the windowsill of his room and lost his balance. He fell into the void and died on the night of Monday into Tuesday morning.

The spokesperson for Mustaro said that it was an accident and there was no violence involved. The mayor was in his room with a colleague, who witnessed the tragic fall. The police have excluded the hypothesis of an aggression or a suicide attempt.

The visit

The mayor was part of a Polish delegation that was visiting Sardinia for some institutional meetings. The delegation included representatives of local authorities, entrepreneurs and journalists from Poland. They had arrived in Sardinia on Sunday and were scheduled to leave on Wednesday.

The purpose of the visit was to strengthen the cooperation and exchange between Poland and Sardinia, especially in the fields of tourism, culture and economy. The delegation had met with the president of the Sardinian regional council, Gianfranco Ganau, and other local officials on Monday. They had also visited some cultural and natural attractions of the island.

The reactions

The news of the mayor's death shocked both Poland and Sardinia. The Polish embassy in Rome expressed its condolences and offered its assistance to the family and friends of the deceased. The embassy also thanked the Italian authorities for their collaboration and support.

The president of the Sardinian regional council, Gianfranco Ganau, said that he was deeply saddened by the tragic event and that he had a cordial and constructive meeting with the mayor on Monday. He said that he hoped that the relations between Poland and Sardinia would continue to grow despite this tragedy.

The mayor of Cagliari, Paolo Truzzu, also expressed his sorrow and solidarity with the Polish delegation and the family of the mayor. He said that he was shocked by the incident and that he hoped that it would not affect the friendship between Cagliari and Poland.

The mayor

Radosław Grzegorz Ostakiewicz was the mayor of Jaworze, a village in southern Poland, since 2018. He was a doctor of economics and a lecturer at the University of Bielsko-Biała. He was also the president of the Local Action Group "Ziemia Bielska", an association that promotes rural development and cooperation.

Ostakiewicz was known for his commitment to improving the quality of life and public services in his municipality. He initiated and supported various projects in the fields of education, culture, sports, tourism, ecology and social welfare. He was also active in fostering international relations and partnerships with other regions and countries.

Ostakiewicz was married and had two children. He was described by his colleagues and friends as a kind, honest and competent person who cared deeply about his community. His sudden death has left a void in the hearts of many who knew him and worked with him.
