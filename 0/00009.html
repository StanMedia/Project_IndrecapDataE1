Russia's military might under strain amid Ukraine conflict, report says

Valentyn Ogirenko/Reuters/File

A new analysis suggests that Russia is struggling to produce and maintain new weapons, while relying on its older arsenal to wage war in Ukraine.

Russia faces challenges in modernizing its military

Russia's military has suffered significant losses of equipment and personnel in the ongoing conflict with Ukraine, according to a new report by the Center for Strategic and International Studies (CSIS), a Washington-based think tank.

The report, titled "Russia's Military Capabilities in 2023: Implications for European Security", estimates that Russia has lost almost 10,000 units of key equipment such as tanks, trucks, artillery pieces and aerial drones since the war began in 2014.

The report also cites Western sanctions, corruption, industrial inefficiency and demographic decline as factors that have hampered Russia's efforts to modernize its military and develop new weapons systems.

"Russia faces serious challenges in producing and maintaining sufficient quantities of modern equipment to equip its forces," the report says.

The report notes that some of Russia's flagship projects, such as the Su-57 stealth fighter jet, the Armata main battle tank and the Sarmat intercontinental ballistic missile, have faced delays, technical problems and cost overruns.

"Russia's ability to field these systems in meaningful numbers and with adequate support remains uncertain," the report says.

Russia still has enough firepower to sustain the war in Ukraine

Despite its difficulties in producing new weapons, Russia still has enough older ones to continue the war in Ukraine, the report says.

The report estimates that Russia has deployed about 100,000 troops, 1,300 tanks, 3,700 armored vehicles, 1,300 artillery systems and 400 aircraft to the border regions with Ukraine.

The report also says that Russia has increased its use of air strikes, missile attacks and electronic warfare against Ukrainian forces and civilian targets.

"Russia retains a significant advantage over Ukraine in terms of conventional military capabilities," the report says.

The report warns that Russia could escalate the conflict further by launching a large-scale offensive across multiple fronts, or by using tactical nuclear weapons as a last resort.

"Russia has demonstrated a willingness to use force to achieve its political objectives in Ukraine and elsewhere," the report says.

The implications for European security and NATO

The report argues that Russia's military capabilities pose a serious threat to European security and stability, and calls for a robust response from NATO and its partners.

The report urges NATO to enhance its deterrence posture by increasing its presence and readiness in Eastern Europe, providing more support to Ukraine and other partner countries, strengthening its cyber and space defenses, and developing credible options for escalation management.

The report also recommends that NATO engage in dialogue with Russia on arms control, crisis prevention and confidence-building measures, while maintaining a firm stance on sanctions and human rights.

"Russia's military capabilities in 2023 will present significant challenges for European security. NATO and its partners must be prepared to counter them effectively," the report concludes.
